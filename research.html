Research

Research Areas

Three core pillars driving our work — biomimetic materials, nanomaterials, and AI-driven materials discovery.

Area 01

Biomimetic Materials

We investigate the self-assembly of DNA and RNA nanomaterials with programmable, responsive properties. By combining principles from structural biology with computational materials design, we engineer molecular architectures that mimic natural biological systems at nanometer precision.

A central thrust is the biomimetic design of materials for phosphorus sustainability — developing bio-inspired capture and recycling strategies informed by molecular-level understanding of phosphate-binding proteins and nanochannel structures.

  • Self-assembly of DNA/RNA nanomaterials with responsive properties
  • Biomimetic design for phosphorus sustainability and nutrient recovery
  • Enzyme engineering and biocatalysis at the molecular scale
  • In silico materials design using DFT and all-atom MD
  • Nanochannel materials from biomolecular magnetic organic frameworks
DNA Nanotechnology Self-Assembly Biocatalysis Phosphorus Sustainability In Silico Design
Area 02

Nanomaterials & Nanoparticles

We develop new molecular modeling approaches for magnetic and metal nanoparticles, enabling predictive understanding of their structure, dynamics, and interactions across multiple length scales. Our multiscale frameworks bridge atomistic resolution with mesoscale behavior.

Our polymer nanocomposite research elucidates how nanoparticle surface chemistry and dispersion state determine bulk mechanical and transport properties, guiding rational material design with reduced experimental iteration.

  • Molecular modeling for magnetic and metal nanoparticles
  • Multiscale modeling from atomistic to mesoscale
  • Polymer nanocomposites and interface engineering
  • Coarse-grained force field development for nanoparticle systems
  • Nanoparticle self-assembly and directed organization
Multiscale Modeling Nanoparticles Magnetic Materials Polymer Nanocomposites Coarse-Grained MD
Area 03

AI & Data Science

We leverage data-science techniques and modern AI architectures to extract insights from heterogeneous materials datasets and accelerate the design–synthesis–characterization loop. Our workflows integrate LLMs, graph neural networks, and Bayesian optimization to make materials discovery more predictive and data-efficient.

A major focus is retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) for scientific literature mining, enabling automated extraction of experimental parameters and materials property trends to drive high-throughput experimental design campaigns.

  • AI-driven workflows for materials discovery and optimization
  • Bayesian optimization for high-throughput experimental design
  • Retrieval-augmented generation for scientific literature mining
  • Graph neural networks for molecular property prediction
  • Large language models for materials informatics
