Teaching & Education

Graduate and undergraduate courses in materials informatics, nanoscale simulation, and biomaterials — offered in person and via distance education at NC State University.

MSE 723

Materials Informatics

Graduate Distance Ed & In Person Machine Learning · Data Science
Course Goals

This course introduces the emergent field of materials informatics and current approaches that employ machine learning and experimental and computational data to accelerate the process of materials optimization, discovery, and development. An emphasis is placed on the practical implementation of machine learning techniques to various materials science problems.

Student Learning Outcomes
Demonstrate an understanding of key materials informatics concepts and components
Explain the relationship between materials and data-driven techniques
Interpret the informatics problems and capabilities associated with different types of materials
Describe available machine learning techniques and materials databases
Identify a machine learning algorithm with the desired properties for a given materials problem
Evaluate existing and emerging ML technologies and analyze trends in data-driven techniques
Related Publication
Thomas J. Oweida, Akhlak Ul-Mahmood, Matthew D. Manning, Sergei Rigin, Yaroslava G. Yingling. MRS Advances 5 (2020) 1–18
Discusses the growing use of materials informatics in academia and industry, highlights the need for educational advances in materials informatics, and describes the implementation of a materials informatics course to equip students with the skills required to succeed in informatics projects.
MSE 721

Nanoscale Simulations and Modeling

Graduate Distance Ed & In Person Nanotechnology · Multiscale Methods
Course Goals

This course is designed to assist engineering students in learning the fundamentals and cutting-edge nature of various simulation methods and their application to nanostructures and nanotechnology. Modeling tools range from accurate first-principles quantum-based methods to multi-scale approaches that combine atomic and continuum modeling. An emphasis is placed on using computational methods for data-driven approaches for the design of new materials.

Student Learning Objectives
Identify and explain the advantages and limitations of various computational techniques
Explain the properties of various nanomaterials and nanosystems
Select appropriate computational methods for specific materials, properties and processing challenges
Evaluate literature based on the use of computational techniques
Design research problems with the use of computational techniques
MSE 485

Biomaterials / MSE 791

Undergrad & Graduate Distance Ed & In Person Biosystems · Drug Delivery · Energy
Course Goals

This course introduces fundamental aspects associated with the synthesis, properties, processing, and application of materials derived from or associated with bio-entities. The course focuses on biomaterials with broad applications beyond medical or clinical uses, emphasizing biological systems' unique machinery and function in the context of desired material outcomes. Topics include:

  • → Differences among classes of biomaterials; toxicity vs. biocompatibility
  • → Bulk vs. surface properties; interactions with different environments
  • → Biomaterials stability and degradation
  • → Sensing and bioelectronics applications
  • → Energy, soft robotics, and responsive materials applications
  • → Drug delivery, multiplexing, and theranostic applications
Student Learning Objectives
Explain the properties of various biomaterials
Select appropriate methods to address specific materials, properties and processing challenges
Apply chemistry and engineering knowledge to solve challenges in biomaterials
Critically review scientific literature and identify areas of research opportunity
